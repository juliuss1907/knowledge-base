# Cynefin Framework

## Definition

Cynefin is a decision framework developed by Dave Snowden at IBM in the late 1990s to help practitioners determine which mode of thinking a situation actually calls for. It distinguishes five domains and provides guidance on how to approach problems differently based on which domain they fall into.

## Key ideas

- Cynefin is not a replacement for systems thinking but a diagnostic tool for knowing when different approaches are needed
- The framework distinguishes five domains: Clear, Complicated, Complex, Chaotic, and Confused
- In the **Complicated** domain, systems thinking excels—there are right answers discoverable through expertise and analysis
- In the **Complex** domain, systems thinking begins to slip—there are no right answers in advance, only emergent ones
- Cause and effect in the Complex domain are only visible in retrospect, not predictable beforehand
- The appropriate response to complexity is not better forecasting but better sensing and faster response
- Many organizational failures stem from treating Complex problems as if they were merely Complicated
