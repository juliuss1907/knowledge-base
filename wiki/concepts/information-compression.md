# Information Compression

## Definition

The process by which detailed information is simplified or condensed during transfer, resulting in unchecked assumptions being propagated throughout an organization. Lossy information transfer contributes to lazy thinking by removing nuance and verification.

## Key ideas

- Lossy information transfer propagates unchecked assumptions
- Simplification during communication removes critical details
- Assumptions travel without verification when compressed
- Contributes to organizational lazy thinking
- Contrast with explicit, hypothesis-driven communication
- See more: Reference to Alexandr Wang's related post on Information Compression
