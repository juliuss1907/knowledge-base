# AI Promises Efficiency, But It's Also Amplifying Labour Inequality
**Source:** The Conversation  
**Link:** https://theconversation.com/ai-promises-efficiency-but-its-also-amplifying-labour-inequality-258772
---
## Tóm tắt
AI đang reshape cách work tasks được hoàn thành và experience của work itself, nhưng tác động không đều — amplify existing inequalities.
---
## Uneven Impact
**Low-wage/entry-level roles:**
- **14x more likely** bị displaced so với higher-wage positions
- Women **1.5x more likely** bị pushed into new occupations
- Targets: customer service, retail, admin, warehousing, food service
- Examples: UPS 20,000 layoffs (Figure AI robots), Amazon 14,000 corporate cuts, Microsoft 6,000 engineers (AI writes 30% code)
**High-wage knowledge workers:**
- **75%** sử dụng AI tools
- **66%** average productivity increase
- Consultants: **40%+ higher quality** work với AI
- Some save **1/3 work hours**
---
## Complementarity & Education
- Canadian data: AI benefit cao nhất khi jobs involve "complementary" tasks
- **Strongly tied to education** — highest ở graduate degrees, declines as education drops
- Benefits flow **disproportionately to higher-educated, high-income** professionals
---
## Upskilling Barriers
| Group | Upskilling Rate |
|---|---|
| Computer-related occupations | **75%** |
| Office admin, food service, production, transportation | **<33%** |
→ Lower socioeconomic workers lack financial means, time, organizational support
---
## Digital Divide
**Advantaged:**
- High-income, white-collar, urban, well-resourced institutions
- Reliable internet, AI tools, formal training
- AI literacy through education & job training
**Disadvantaged:**
- Manual jobs, rural, low-income, marginalized communities, older age
- Lack stable connectivity, updated tech, formal training
- More vulnerable to AI-related job threats
---
## Conclusion
> "Without equitable access to upskilling, reskilling, training, digital resources and AI literacy, the technology can deepen disparities."
Closing gaps essential for AI to benefit society broadly and equitably.
