# Philosopher Syndrome

## Definition

A condition where otherwise sensible people fall prey to lazy thinking when isolated from data and outcomes. As organizations grow and functions become siloed, individuals lose automatic access to feedback loops that enforce active thinking.

## Key ideas

- Sensible people become susceptible to lazy thinking without data exposure
- Occurs when organizations grow and functions become siloed
- Individuals lose automatic connection to metrics that enforce active thinking
- Solution is to always understand metrics, understand reality
- Best practice is to hold oneself accountable to change based on data
- Core principle: "embrace reality and deal with it" (Dalioan credo)
- Isolation from outcomes is a hidden organizational crutch
- Prevents learning from actual results and iterating effectively
