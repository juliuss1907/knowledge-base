# Organizational Incrementalism

## Definition

A trap where organizations make changes too small to yield large impacts, while convincing themselves they are making meaningful progress. In technology, meaningful progress requires leaps forward, not just incremental improvements.

## Key ideas

- Making changes too small to yield significant impact
- Self-deception about progress toward goals
- In technology, progress comes from meaningful changes that leap forward product power
- Small incremental changes as primary contribution leads to mediocrity
- Incremental changes are necessary but insufficient for success
- Without big leaps forward, incrementalism means certain death
- Organizations may confuse activity with progress
- Must balance incremental improvements with transformative changes
- Successful periods often result from deep active thinking, not just incremental work
