# Active Thinking

## Definition

A rigorous decision-making approach that forces clear, verifiable hypotheses and obtains data to validate or refute them. Active thinking requires asking questions, challenging assumptions, and identifying real bottlenecks and constraints to sort through noise and identify root causes of failure.

## Key ideas

- Formulates clear, verifiable hypotheses
- Obtains data that validates or refutes hypotheses
- Asks questions and challenges assumptions consistently
- Identifies real bottlenecks and constraints
- Sorts through noise to identify reasons for failure
- Requires rigorous diagnosis of each cause of failure
- Force of will alone is insufficient—must be coupled with active thinking
- Embodied by the scientific method and Dalio's "embrace reality and deal with it"
- Necessary for organizations to maintain efficacy as they scale
- Core to Scale AI's credo: "Intellectual rigor, open mind"
