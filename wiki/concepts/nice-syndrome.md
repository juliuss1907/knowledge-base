# Nice Syndrome

## Definition

An organizational dysfunction where people avoid challenging others' beliefs because they fear being perceived as rude rather than righteous. Politeness becomes a crutch that prevents rigorous thinking on important matters.

## Key ideas

- People tend to be excessively polite in large groups
- Failing to challenge beliefs due to perceived rudeness is lazy thinking
- On important matters, it's imperative to uphold precision and verifiability
- Open-mindedness is required—being receptive to challenges is part of active thinking
- Not about pedantically correcting every error, but addressing ideas that matter
- For important ideas, lazy thinking is fatal to organizational success
- Prevents organizations from identifying and solving real problems
