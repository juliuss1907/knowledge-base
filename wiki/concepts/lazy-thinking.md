# Lazy Thinking

## Definition

A decision-making mode characterized by reliance on broad, imprecise beliefs that are either impossible to test or not rigorously validated against data. Lazy thinking accepts intuitive statements as true without proper assessment and favors convenient "truths" that conform to existing paths rather than confronting reality.

## Key ideas

- Makes decisions through untested or untestable broad beliefs
- Assumes intuitive statements are true without data validation
- Believes convenient "truths" that match current trajectory
- Primary symptom is repeated, chronic failure—often unrecognized
- Common in large organizations that have become successful and rest on their laurels
- Organizations lose not because they don't want to win, but because they forget how to win
- Can be mistaken for wisdom or experience in established organizations
- Contrast with active thinking which uses verifiable hypotheses
