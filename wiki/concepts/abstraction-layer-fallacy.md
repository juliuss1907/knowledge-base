# Abstraction Layer Fallacy

## Definition

The error of believing that clean abstraction layers in system diagrams or state machines represent reality. Organizations confuse simplified models with the actual complex, convoluted world where edge cases break abstractions.

## Key ideas

- Organizations believe in clean abstraction layers and confuse them for reality
- System diagrams and state machines present a pure view of the world
- The real world is extremely complex and convoluted
- Almost every abstraction layer has edge cases that break it
- Believing in abstraction purity is lazy thinking
- Cannot trust that the "black box" will always work
- When abstractions fail, those who trusted them are unprepared to deal with it
- Requires understanding implementation details, not just interfaces
- Particularly dangerous in technology organizations with complex systems
