# Complicated vs Complex

## Definition

The distinction between complicated and complex systems is fundamental to modern systems thinking. While both have many parts and interactions, they respond to analysis and intervention in fundamentally different ways—requiring different management approaches.

## Key ideas

- **Complicated systems**: Many parts, many interactions, but parts follow rules that don't change as you observe them
- **Complex systems**: Agents inside adapt, learn, and respond to interventions by changing behavior—the rules evolve as the system runs
- Complicated systems yield to analysis—you can model them, develop plans that will hold
- Complex systems resist prediction—no static map fully captures what's happening because the act of mapping changes the territory
- Complicated: cause and effect are discoverable through analysis; Complex: cause and effect only visible in retrospect
- Most management tools were designed for complicated problems, not complex ones
- Treating complex problems as merely complicated is a common source of organizational failure
- Cynefin framework provides diagnostic clarity for determining which domain you're actually in
